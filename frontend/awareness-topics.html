Cyber Safety

The internet has made life convenient but also created new dangers. Cyber crimes in India are rising rapidly. Protect yourself and your family by staying informed and alert.

Online Privacy

Protect your personal information and digital identity at all times.

  • Use strong, unique passwords for every account
  • Enable two-factor authentication
  • Never share personal info publicly
  • Be careful about location sharing

Cyberbullying

Online harassment and bullying is a punishable offense under IT Act 2000.

  • Document all evidence — screenshots
  • Report on the platform immediately
  • Block harassers without engaging
  • File FIR at cyber crime police station

Financial Fraud

UPI fraud, phishing, and online scams are common. Stay alert.

  • Never share OTP or PIN with anyone
  • Verify before clicking unknown links
  • Use only official banking apps
  • Report fraud immediately to your bank

Child Online Safety

Protect children from online predators, inappropriate content, and exploitation.

  • Use parental controls on all devices
  • Educate children about online dangers
  • Monitor online friends and activities
  • Teach children to say no to strangers online

Legal Framework

Indian law provides strong protections against cyber crimes.

  • IT Act 2000 — Cyber Crimes
  • Section 66C — Identity Theft
  • Section 66E — Privacy Violation
  • POCSO Act for child exploitation

Social Evils Awareness

Social evils are harmful practices that harm individuals and communities. Awareness and education are the most powerful tools to eliminate these practices from society.

Dowry System

Dowry is illegal, harmful, and a leading cause of domestic violence and femicide in India.

  • Dowry Prohibition Act 1961
  • IPC 304B — Dowry Death
  • Report demands to police immediately
  • Section 498A — Cruelty by husband

Domestic Violence

Domestic violence includes physical, emotional, sexual, and financial abuse within the home.

  • Protection of Women from DV Act 2005
  • Seek Protection Orders through court
  • Contact One Stop Centres for help
  • No victim is ever at fault

Caste Discrimination

Caste-based discrimination is unconstitutional and a punishable offense in India.

  • SC/ST Prevention of Atrocities Act
  • Article 17 — Abolition of Untouchability
  • Report caste crimes to NHRC
  • Know your rights as equal citizens

Child Marriage

Child marriage robs children of their childhood, education, and health.

  • Prohibition of Child Marriage Act 2006
  • Legal age: 18 for girls, 21 for boys
  • Call 1098 (Childline) to report
  • Child marriage is void under law

Substance Abuse

Alcohol and drug addiction devastate individuals, families, and communities.

  • NARCIND — National Addiction Helpline
  • De-addiction centers in every district
  • NIMHANS for psychiatric support
  • Early intervention saves lives

Child Protection

Every child deserves to grow up in a safe, loving environment free from abuse, exploitation, and neglect. Adults have a collective responsibility to protect children.

Good Touch / Bad Touch

Teaching children about body autonomy and consent from an early age is critical for protection.

  • Private body parts are always private
  • No secret touching is ever okay
  • Tell a trusted adult immediately
  • It is never the child's fault

Legal Protections

Indian law provides strong, comprehensive protections for all children.

  • POCSO Act 2012 — Child Sexual Offenses
  • RTE Act — Right to Education
  • Juvenile Justice Act 2015
  • Child Labour Prohibition Act

Online Safety for Kids

The internet poses unique risks for children including grooming, exploitation, and cyberbullying.

  • Enable parental controls on all devices
  • Monitor apps and social media use
  • Teach children about online strangers
  • Create a safe space to report problems

Recognizing Abuse

Early recognition of abuse signs can prevent further harm and help survivors heal.

  • Sudden behavioral changes in children
  • Withdrawal from family and friends
  • Unexplained injuries or fear of adults
  • Regression in development milestones

Education Rights

Every child in India has the constitutional right to free and compulsory education.

  • RTE Act — Free education ages 6–14
  • Mid-day meal scheme for nutrition
  • Government scholarships for all levels
  • Report school denial to DEO

Mental Health

Mental health is just as important as physical health. Over 150 million Indians suffer from mental health conditions, yet most never seek help due to stigma. Let us break this barrier together.

Depression and Anxiety

Common but serious conditions that affect millions. Early help leads to full recovery.

  • Persistent sadness for over 2 weeks
  • Loss of interest in daily activities
  • Excessive worry, panic attacks
  • Seek professional help — it works

Suicide Prevention

Suicide is preventable. Listening without judgment can save a life.

  • Take all mentions of suicide seriously
  • Listen with empathy, without judgment
  • Remove access to means if possible
  • Contact iCall: 9152987821 immediately

Stress Management

Unmanaged stress leads to physical and mental illness. Build healthy coping habits.

  • Regular exercise and physical activity
  • Mindfulness and meditation practice
  • Adequate sleep — 7 to 8 hours daily
  • Talk to someone you trust

Breaking the Stigma

Stigma prevents people from seeking help. Our words and attitudes matter.

  • Never dismiss someone's feelings
  • Use respectful, non-stigmatizing language
  • Share accurate mental health information
  • Support those who seek treatment

Seeking Help

Professional mental health support is effective, accessible, and available across India.

  • Government hospitals have psychiatry OPD
  • NIMHANS — National Mental Health Institute
  • Vandrevala Foundation 24/7 helpline
  • Online therapy through iCall, YourDost
